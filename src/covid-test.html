All Covid-19 Tests

What is a PCR Test?

PCR is a short form of real-time "Polymerase Chain Reaction" and it is the gold standard and most accurate laboratory test for confirming the diagnosis of COVID 19 infection. It is specifically designed to detect the presence of the tinest amount of genetic material of the SARS-CoV-2 Virus. Only individuals that are actively infected with the Virus can have the genetic material detected in their Swab samples.

The PCR test is conducted through multi-stage processes in specially equipped laboratories, therefore the results are usually not available until some hours after the swabs arrive at the laboratories.

What is an Antigen Test?

COVID 19 Antigen test which is also called "Lateral Flow Tests" are immunoassays based tests that check for the COVID 19 viral protein by application of individuals samples directly into the assay’s extraction buffer or reagent. It is usually used at the point-of-care and returns results in approximately 15–30 minutes. Antigen tests for SARS-CoV-2 are generally less sensitive than real-time real-time Polymerase chain reaction (RT-PCR) amount of genetic material of the SARS-CoV-2 Virus.

Antigen tests perform best in symptomatic people and within a certain number of days since onset of symptom because their viral load is generally highest at this point. This test may also reflect the likelihood that an individula has been exposed to a person infected with COVID-19.

What is an Antibody Test?

COVID 19 Antibody test are used to see if individual's body may have built up antibodies for COVID-19 and does not indicate if the virus is currently active in your system. Antibodies can take several days or weeks to develop after an infection and may stay in the blood for several weeks or more after recovery. Samples for antibody tests are typically blood from a finger stick, or blood drawn by your doctor or other medical personnel.

Antibody tests should not be used to diagnose an active COVID-19 infection. It is usually used at the point-of-care and returns results in approximately 15–30 minutes.
